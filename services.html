TESTING OF POTABLE DRINKING WATER / DOMESTIC WATER ON BOARD SHIPS

MLC A3.2 requires, 'Each Member shall ensure that, ships that fly its flag, carry on board an serve food and drinking water of appropriate quality.

MLC B5.1.4 (8) recommends, 'Inspectors provided with proper credentials under the national law should at a minimum be empowered (e) to take or remove, for the purpose of analysis, samples of products, cargo, drinking water, provisions, materials and substances used or handled;'

TECHNOPORT USA, carries out testing of Potable Water Samples Analysis from vessels, based on some of the parameters specified by the WHO. Parameters tested for include, Potability, Color, pH, Conductivity, Hardness, Turbidity, Lead, Copper, Cadmium, Iron, Nickel, Zinc, Chromium, Chlorine, Chlorine Dioxide, Escherichia coli, Total Coliform and Total Legionella.

Drinking water should be examined / identified for the highest potential risk to avoid further problems. Where water is used for human consumption on board ship, it must be potable. It is essential to ensure clean & healthy water for consumption onboard ship.

Laboratory testing of water samples from the ship are carried out in accordance with ISO/IEC 17025.

SEWAGE EFFLUENT WATER TESTING

We carry out the test of the sewage effluent, according to the protocol requirements and standards set forth by Marpol 73/78 & resolutions MEPC.2(VI), MEPC.159(55) & MEPC.227(64) & on board 'Sewage pollution prevention certification' (for all vessels whose sewage treatment plants were in production after 1st Jan 2010).

Parameters Tested are:

  • pH
  • Total Suspended Solids
  • Chemical Oxygen Demand (C.O.D)
  • Biochemical Oxygen Demand (B.O.D)
  • Fecal Coliform

Sewage treatment plants installed prior to 1 January 2010 on ships other than passenger ships operating in MARPOL Annex IV special areas and intending to discharge treated sewage effluent into the sea, should comply with resolution MEPC.2(VI). Adopted on 3 December 1976

Sewage treatment plants installed prior to 1 January 2016 and on or after 1 January 2010, should comply with resolution MEPC159(55). Adopted on 13 October 2006

Sewage treatment plants installed on or after 1 January 2016 on ships, should comply with resolution MEPC 227(64). Adopted on 5 October 2012

Analysis methods, including indicative methods, listed in the guidance on ballast water sampling and analysis (BWM.2/Circ.42/Rev.1) may be applied. A test facility may use a portable test kit, given that the reliability and accuracy of this test kit has been validated.
